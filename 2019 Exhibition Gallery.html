Talking a Look at Education from a New Light

Schools are where most people have spent their childhoods in, where they were sometimes oppressed but also given joy through learning. Through this exhibition, the members of Canvas Studio have demonstrated their different views of school and education as honestly as possible. After much contemplation, we decided to hold an exhibition with the theme “School” in light of showcasing our work as students who go about their daily lives in school. We wanted our audience to Look at education, school, and the overall atmosphere of the Korean education system from a different perspective, hence the title “Look: Taking a Look at Education from a New Light.”

We had two successful exhibitions last year at Bugil High School with the theme “Nature,” and this year, we decided to take a step further to make our work worth something. We expanded our club to include more people, a greater variety of artwork, and a new goal: to help the community children to approach art class outside of the classes in school, most of which are created to follow a set curriculum. We want the students to learn that there is much more to life than studying – that there is much more joy in taking advantage of artistic expression amidst their rigorous school life. Sometimes, we have to take a look at education from a completely different perspective. Right now, we are conducting a fundraiser and we will be using 100% of the money to fund the materials for art classes, which will be held at the Shining Children’s Center in Cheonan. Any leftover donations will be used to provide extra art material for the children’s center to use whenever they need to.

All of our hard work has been formulated through thick and thin, through midterms and SATs and ACTs. However, all of us defied all the odds by creating masterpieces that demonstrate that school isn’t all about studying, but it is about striving to reach our true desires, which, in this case, was art. Through this exhibition, we hope that all of you can reflect upon what school and education has been to you, especially if you know what the Korean education system is like. We hope that the 15 different perspectives will guide you through the minds of current junior high students in Korea.




A Very Modest Proposal (Team Cheolsoo Runs)

This production alludes to the ironic situation of the Korean education system in which the students sleep in class and study at night. Students are so preoccupied with going to extracurricular classes (hagwons or academies) after school, they spend most of their time in school either napping or finishing their hagwon homework. Through the advertisement of the “Wakey-Wakey,” which is a student torturing device to keep them awake, we wanted to satirize the reality of the Korean education system – a system that fails to provide proper education for students by creating such a competitive environment that everyone (with money) resorts to classes outside of school. What is the point of going to school?

it is always nice to fall into your own virtual world. My art shows two different points of views, one on the right side in the third and the other in the first person. Animal School

A school is a place of collective learning, it is a place where education is handed down from generations to generations. This drawing portrays a similar system amongst animals where animals are also collectively learning -- just like humans.

인간의 학교는 이전 세대의 지식이 전달되는 가장 대표적인 시스템이다. 인간들을 동물들로 표현하여 동물들도 유사한 형태의 교육 시스템이 존재할 것이라는 가정하에 작품을 상상하며 그렸다.

Iron Man's Death and Retirement (Wooyoung Kim)

In the Endgame, Iron Man dies in his final fight with Thanos because his contract ended with Marvel. I wanted to commemorate Iron Man’s death and retirement with this sculpture.

이번 어벤져스 엔드게임에서 아이언맨이 타노스와의 마지막 전투에서 핑거스냅을 날려 죽게 된다. 이는 아이언맨이 마블과의 계약이 끝났기 때문이다. 그래서 아이언맨이 죽은 기일을 위해서, 그리고 로다주가 마블의 은퇴식을 생각해서 작품을 만들었다.



Drowning (Nayoung Ha)

A classroom is a place of friendship and joy, but to some it is a place of loneliness and fear -- just like being alone in a fishbowl. Our society more than often tries to cover these “misfits” without really asking them why they feel excluded. However, this feeling of being left out happens to everybody, not only to a few of our students. By drawing this picture, I wanted to send a message to those who feel excluded and tell them that they are not the only ones feeling that way.

교실은 학생들이 다른 학생들과 친목을 다지는, 학업에 지친 마음을 위로하는 장소지만 동시에 학생들이 외로움과 답답함을 느끼는 장소이기도 하다. 우리 사회는 너무나도 자주 이런 학생들을 잘 적응하지 못하는 학생으로 분류하여 무시하며 덮으려고만 한다. 하지만 현실에서 이 답답한 마음은 누구나 한 번은 느끼는 흔한 감정이다. 이 작품을 통해서 학교생활 중 홀로 어항속에 갇혀있는듯한 숨막히는 답답함과 아무에게도 말할 수 없는 소외감을 나타내고자 한다. 그리고 그들에게 혼자만 그런 것이 아니라는 위로를 건내고자 한다.

Colorful

The flowers inside the classroom represent the individuals’ dreams and the colorful landscape outside the classroom windows represent the world – a world that requires diversity of experience. I wanted to show that students have to try hard even outside the classroom to achieve their dreams.

학생들의 다양한 꿈을 학생들이 가지고 있는 꽃으로 표현했다. 교실 밖이 보이는 창문의 풍경을 작품명처럼 알록달록하게하여 학생들이 꿈을 이루기 위해서는 교실안에서만이 아닌 바깥세상에서도 노력을 해야 한다는 것을 전달하고자 했다.



Educator 1.1 (Nayoung Ha)

The current Korean education system expects every unique individual to become one specific type of person – a hard working, diligent scholar with a caring heart and a brilliant mind. Though from a long-shot it seems as if the system is trying to help individuals pursue their dreams, the Korean education system still lacks the flexibility to embrace students as individuals instead of viewing them by merely their KSAT scores. By drawing “Educator 1.1” with my tablet, I wanted to bring awareness into how students are treated like factory goods – how schools are trying to fit everybody into one chosen model when everybody’s different.

현재 한국의 교육과정은 다른 개성을 가진 모두가 한 가지의 전형적인 인간상을 따라가는 것을 목표로 한다. 표면적으로는 학생들이 각기 다른 진로를 따라가기 위해 다양한 활동들을 참여하도록 제안하지만 아직 오로지 점수를 받아내기 위해 공부를 해야하는 국내 교육의 근본적인 문제는 해결되지 않고 있다. 이에 영감을 받아 이 작품을 그리게 되었다. 학생들이 “제작"되는 과정을 공장에서 인형들을 맞혀진 메뉴얼로 만들어지는 것처럼 희화화했고 학생들의 생각마저도 미리 짜여진 소프트웨어를 통해서 정해지는 것처럼 표현을 하고자 했다. Educator 1.1을 통해서 학생들이 마주해야하는 숨막히는 학교생활과 우리나라 교육의 현실을 전달하고자 한다.



Monochrome (Jane Lee)

Amidst the competitive education system of Korea, students strive so hard to appear “different” from one another in order to appeal to the admission officers of middle school, high school, and college. Even when applying for foreign colleges, students often involve themselves in excessive amounts of extracurriculars – they want to be “colorful.” However, I wanted to show that when we look at students with a monochrome filter, they aren't all that different. I wanted the world to stop judging students by their grades and extracurriculars. I thought that with a monochrome filter, a world of black and white, we'd be able to see students as students – individuals who have a life other than studying. Students do more than just study – they do music, they do art, they do sports, they have fun, they fight for their rights, they do much more than the stereotypical expectations of what students should do in Korea.

학생들 사이의 경쟁을 촉진시키는 우리나라의 교육은 학생마다 ‘특별한' 면모를 보여달라고 자주 요구한다. 이 때문에 학생들은 학원, 봉사, 외부 활동 등으로 화려한 스펙을 쌓아가려고 애를 쓰고, 학생들의 학창시절은 그렇게 무의미하게 지나간다. 하지만 흑백으로 모든 학생들을 보면 화려한 상장들과 트로피들 모두 한낱 회색 종이와 하얀 컵일 뿐이다. 흑백촬영을 하여 지나친 경쟁으로 서로가 서로보다 더 화려한 “색깔”을 가지고 있어야한다는 부담감을 덜고 학생들 본인들이 그냥 있는대로의 삶을 행복하게 받아들이면 된다는 메세지를 전달하고자 한다. 학생들이 오로지 스펙을 쌓기 위한 활동이 아닌 자신이 진심으로 즐기는 음악, 미술, 체육, 인권운동, 동아리 활동 등을 하며 한 번 밖에 없는 청춘을 즐겁고 유의미하게 보냈으면 하는 마음에 사진 모음을 만들어봤다.

A Swan Chasing its Dreams (Areum Park)

Inside a classroom, there are students with dreams of their own. Despite rough beginnings, they all grow to achieve their dreams, just like how the Ugly Duckling became a beautiful swan. I wanted to emphasize the students’ growth in schools.

학교에 각자 다양한 꿈이있는 학생들이 여러명 모여있다. 미운 오리 새끼가 자신의 꿈을 이루어 커가며 우아한 백조가 된 것처럼 우리도 학교에서 꿈을 이루기 위해 성장한 것을 모여있는 백조들으로 표현했다.



My Room (Jieun Kang)

When looking at school from a different perspective, a school is very quiet, but loud at the same time. This raging silence and ruckus resembles my own room. My room is where I am also comforted and where I explore my creativity. Through art, a mode of creative expression, I wanted to express the similarity of the class’room’ and my ‘room.’

기존의 틀에 박힌 학교가 아닌 새로운 관점에서 보는 학교는 상당히 조용하지만, 시끄러운 면모를 갖췄다. 생각했을때 조용하지만, 시끄러운 모습을 가춘 공간은 학교만이 아닌 내가 쓰는 '방' 또한 그렇다. 학교와 내 방의 공통점은 이 것 뿐만이 아니라, 나에게 편안함과 개성을 찾아 갈 수 있는 공간이다. 때문에, 나의 개성을 한껏 표현을 할 수 있는 매개체인 그림에 내가 학교에서 받은 이미지를 내 '방'이라 생각하여 그림에 담아내었다.
